About Oceanology

Oceanology is the scientific study of the oceans and their phenomena. It encompasses a wide range of topics, including marine biology, oceanography, marine geology, and marine ecology.

The oceans cover more than 70% of the Earth's surface and play a crucial role in regulating the planet's climate, providing habitat for countless marine species, and supporting various human activities such as fishing, transportation, and recreation.

Oceanologists study various aspects of the oceans, including their physical properties such as temperature, salinity, and currents, as well as their biological diversity and ecological interactions. Understanding the oceans is essential for addressing global challenges such as climate change, pollution, and overfishing.
